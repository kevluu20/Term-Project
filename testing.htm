Testing

SAT

Section Minutes Given Number of Questions
Reading 65 52
Writing and Language 35 44
Math 80 58
Essay (Optional) 50 1
Total 3 hours, 50 minutes (with essay) 154(+ 1 essay prompt)

The SAT is a college entrance exam that is administrated by the College Board. It is a multiple-choice exam that has an optional essay at the end that is recommended but not requried. The score for the exam ranges from 400 being the lowest to 1600 being the highest. Different colleges will have different averages for the SAT so that students have a good idea of whether the school will consider them. Most high school students take the SAT during their junior year of highschool and during the beginning of senior year. The exam can be taken multiple times, however, 3 times maximum is recommended.

The SAT is broken up into 2 sections, which include Math and the Evidence-based Reading and Writing. Each section has specific topics that will be found within the quesitons. Be sure to look at practice questions online or from books to familiarize yourself with the material.

ACT

English 45 75
Math 60 60
Reading 35 40
Science 35 40
Essay (Optional) 40 1
Total 3 hours, 35 minutes (with essay) 215(+ 1 essay prompt)

The ACT, like the SAT, is a college extrance exam that is taken during junior and senior year of highschool. Students can choose to take either the SAT, ACT, or both to see which one is better. The score for the ACT ranges from 1 being the lowest and 36 being the highest. The ACT includes a Math, Reading, Writing, and Science section. The Science section is used to test students' analysis skills and critical thinking. There are practice problems and exams online that students can use to help prepare them for the real exam. Time management is very important for exams like the ACT and SAT. Make sure you have time to complete the entire exam and check over your work if possible.

Should you take the SAT or the ACT?

Both exams generally cover the same material and schools often don't have a preference when it comes to what exam to take. This ultimately comes down to what types of questions you prefer and and the format of the exam. Take the practice exams of both the SAT and ACT and recognize the differences. Both exams have a different pace, feel, and look so it is essential that you choose the exam that will maximize your performance and score.

Top 5 Testing Tips

  1. Answer questions you know first

  2. Eliminate the obvious incorrect answers

  3. If you don't know, make an educated guess

  4. Watch out for time

  5. Practice, practice, and more practice
